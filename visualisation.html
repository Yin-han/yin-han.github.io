Justification

1. Key Visualisation Choices & Interaction Benefits

  • 1.1 Revealing Differences in Sleep Timing by Age — time slider (8 intervals) for exploration.
  • 1.2 Highlighting rhythmic/responsibility differences (teens later; 46–60 more stable).
  • 1.3 Analysing relationship between bedtime and sleep quality — coordinated comparison.
  • 1.4 Supporting data-driven causal exploration with two linked views.
  • 1.5 Personalised exploration matches Shneiderman’s mantra.
  • 1.6 Coordinated multi-chart storytelling: When → Who → Why (Setting → Aha).
  • 1.7 Time slider + colour encoding (blue sleep %, red quality) for dimensional comparison.

2. Interactions & Animations (Annotated)

  • P1 Story navigation with arrows — View manipulation.
  • P2 Autoplay slider — Temporal animation.
  • P3 Tooltips for exact values — Details on demand.
  • P4–P5 Flip card animation in “Looking Closer”.
  • P6 Colour emphasis on min/max values.

3. Design Theory References

  • Shneiderman (1996) – Information Seeking Mantra.
  • Heer & Shneiderman (2012) – Taxonomy of Interactive Dynamics.
  • Tufte – Graphical integrity & data-ink ratio.
  • Munzner – Visual perception & colour encoding.
